Overview

A grim-dark survival RTS: hold a Lantern outpost against echo-hunting Drowned after the deep ocean rose.

Premise

  • Industrial-medieval crossed with WWI-naval. The ocean climbed out of the trenches and never receded.
  • Defend the inland edge; coral encroaches year by year; survival is the only victory condition.

Canonical GDD

  • The exhaustive spec is the GDD at /brackish-rising/: systems, the full tech tree, walls/gates, and Numbers tables.

Goals

What the game must be true to.

Design goals

  • Sonic Pressure is central. Anything that hums draws the Drowned in; quiet colonies live.
  • Heavier combat. Smaller groups of high-HP enemies, longer fights; each unit lost is a real loss.
  • Its own world. Sibling to Here Be Hordes; the two do not cross-contaminate.

Decisions

Locked decisions (full set in the GDD).

Locked decisions

  • § Forked from a Hordes alt-direction and now standalone.
  • § Hum-zone mechanics: inside the Lantern hum, Drowned are slowed, melee is softened, Siren summons cannot propagate, friendlies regen.
  • § Gates are 1x3 passable-center occupants; friendlies cross, enemies are blocked even while animating open.
  • § Pathing split: units use a custom A* (iso_grid.find_path) with diagonal-only-if-clear; enemies use NavigationAgent2D over a baked navmesh.

Systems

How it is built.

Engine

  • Godot 4.6, true 2:1 isometric, 32x16 tile size, 1920x1080 viewport-stretch, three zoom tiers.
  • Maps bounded to the local watershed; an off-map walkable staging band with a hard push-back wall.

Tech tree

  • A deep doctrine tree: per-unit lines (Scout, Rifleman, Tommy, Trench Sweeper, Marksman, Harpoonist) plus category trees (Offense, Fortification, Economy, Lantern) and exclusive/standalone nodes.
  • Capstones reshape an army (for example pure-Marksman line-deletion, pure-Trench wave-clear).
