Privacy policy

YesAndBudget is a local-first personal-finance application. This page describes what data the application handles, where it lives, and how to delete it. Last updated 2026-06-08.

What we collect

Nothing. YesAndBudget does not collect, store, or transmit consumer financial data to any operator-controlled server. There is no telemetry. There is no analytics SDK. There is no backend service that receives a copy of your data.

The application runs entirely on the consumer's own device. The source is published at github.com/TheProphetKane/yesandbudget and is auditable.

What the application stores on your device

When you use the application:

  • Transactions, accounts, categories, and rules you create or import are stored in a SQLite database at data/yesandbudget.db on your computer.
  • Pre-import snapshots of the database are stored at data/backups/. These are local backup copies used for one-click rollback of failed imports.
  • Plaid credentials, if you connect a bank via Plaid, are stored at .plaid-token (your Plaid client_id and secret) and at plaid_items.access_token (one access token per linked institution) inside the SQLite database. These remain on your device.
  • Plaid sync metadata (timestamps, cursor positions, counts of imported transactions) is stored in the plaid_sync_log table inside the SQLite database.

All of the above resides on the encrypted volume of your own machine. None is copied to any external server we operate.

What goes out over the network

Only when you connect a bank via Plaid, the application makes outbound HTTPS calls to production.plaid.com using your Plaid credentials. These calls retrieve your bank transactions from Plaid into your local database. Plaid's own privacy policy applies to your relationship with Plaid: plaid.com/legal.

The application's internal HTTP server binds to your computer's loopback interface only. It does not accept connections from any other device on your network or from the public internet.

Cookies, trackers, third-party analytics

None of the above. The application does not include any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking pixels.

How to delete your data

You can delete your data at any time. Three options, in increasing order of finality:

  1. Per-record deletion from inside the application. The Transactions, Accounts, and Categories pages each let you delete individual records.
  2. Per-Plaid-link removal from the Settings page. Removes the link with your bank, invalidates the access token on Plaid's side, and either restores or permanently deletes the Plaid-imported records per your choice.
  3. Full factory reset from the Settings page, under "Delete all my data". This wipes the entire SQLite database, the .plaid-token file, and the data/backups/ directory. No copy of your data survives.

There is no operator-side copy that survives a deletion because the data never reaches an operator-side system.
